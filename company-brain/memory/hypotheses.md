# Working Hypotheses

Status: all unvalidated. Customer interviews this week decide.

---

## H1 — India efficiency angle is dead

**Claim:** "AI saves time / reduces cost" fails as a pitch to Indian CA firms.
- Articles earn <10k INR/month, do ~70% of execution work
- No labor cost pain to relieve
**Status:** Assumed true. Not yet confirmed by owner interviews.
**Would reverse if:** An owner names labor cost as a top pain unprompted.

**Implied real pains to test in India:**
- Partner capacity — seniors who sign off can't scale
- Client acquisition — ICAI bans advertising; firms can't grow marketing-led
- Quality consistency across cheap labor pool
- Partner review burden

---

## H2 — Three wedge candidates (unconverged)

**Wedge A — Services-led embed-and-learn**
Free/cheap pilot, learn the workflow, productize later.
Risk: turns into a consulting firm. Harvey-style $100M burn before product is the cautionary tale.

**Wedge B — US lead-gen agent for accounting firms**
Crawl funding rounds, CFO hires → surface new-accounting-need companies to US CPA firms.
Risk: looks like Clay/Apollo. Defensibility needs accounting-specific signal taxonomy.

**Wedge C — Workflow automation**
Automate specific high-impact CA workflows.
Risk: in India, killed by H1. In US, viable but generic without a clear wedge workflow.

**What interviews need to answer:** Which pain do owners describe with enough urgency to pay for a solution?

---

## H3 — US is the real target market

**Claim:** US CPA owners have 10–30x higher willingness to pay than Indian CA owners for the same token spend.
**Implication:** India = learning lab. US = the actual business.
**Status:** Unvalidated. Running parallel outreach to test which market responds and converts.
