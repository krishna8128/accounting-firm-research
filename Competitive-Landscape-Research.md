## Executive Summary

The market has bifurcated into two dominant theses absorbing the capital:

1. **"Replace NetSuite / QuickBooks with AI-native ERP/GL"** → Rillet, Campfire, Digits, Pennylane, Light, Puzzle
2. **"Sell AI agents INTO accounting firms"** (Top-100 CPA tooling) → Basis, Accrual, Fieldguide, Black Ore, Modus, Aiwyn, Karbon, Canopy, TaxGPT, Filed

**Capital velocity in the last 6 months (Nov 2025 – May 2026):**
Basis ($100M), Accrual ($75M), Fieldguide ($75M Series C), Pennylane ($205M Series E), Rillet ($70M Series B), Modus ($85M Lightspeed-led), Maxima ($41M), Numeric ($51M Series B), Sphere ($21M), Stacks ($23M), Concourse ($12M), Fazeshift ($22M), Zenskar ($15M).

**The "AI replaces junior accountant/auditor/tax associate" thesis is now the dominant narrative.** Sequoia sized the TAM at $50–80B in their Rillet investment thesis. Goldman Sachs Growth Equity entered the category in Feb 2026 (Fieldguide Series C), signaling growth-stage capital validation beyond traditional venture.

## Strategic Takeaways for a Founder Entering the Space

### Where the dry powder lives (just raised, will spend hard in 2026)

- **Basis** $100M (Feb 2026)
- **Accrual** $75M (Feb 2026)
- **Fieldguide** $75M (Feb 2026)
- **Pennylane** $205M (Jan 2026)
- **Modus** $85M (April 2026)
- **Maxima** $41M (Nov 2025)
- **Sphere** $21M (Nov 2025)
- **Fazeshift** $22M (May 2026)
- **Numeric** $51M (Nov 2025)
- **Concourse** $12M (Feb 2026)
- **Stacks** $23M (Feb 2026)
- **Zenskar** $15M (April 2026)

### White spaces / underserved zones

1. **Vertical-specific AI accounting** — Adaptive (construction) and Finaloop (e-commerce) are the only meaningfully funded verticals. Open: healthcare RCM-to-accounting, restaurant/hospitality, professional services, cannabis, agriculture, nonprofits, real estate, manufacturing job-costing.
2. **Geography outside US/EU** — No dominant AI accounting plays in LATAM, SEA (ex-Singapore), Africa, Middle East. India is partially served by Zoho + Khatabook but not on an AI-agent thesis.
3. **CFO copilot for sub-$5M ARR companies** — Concourse and most peers target mid-market+; Truewind/Puzzle/Digits straddle bookkeeping but don't deliver CFO-grade insight.
4. **Specialty audit** — Insurance accounting, banking/credit-union audit, healthcare provider audits — all gaps below Fieldguide's general-purpose play.
5. **Compliance/tax for cross-border digital businesses** — Sphere is the leader but the category is large enough for niche entrants (e.g., creator economy, embedded tax for SaaS billing).

### Defensibility patterns that are working

- **Workflow embed inside firm software** (Fieldguide, Basis, Aiwyn) — switching cost via document repos + workpapers.
- **Big-4 / large-audit distribution** (DataSnipper, MindBridge) — long sales cycle but moat once embedded.
- **AI-native GL as system-of-record** (Rillet, Campfire, Pennylane) — replacing the highest-stickiness software in the stack.
- **Roll-up of services firms with proprietary AI** (Modus) — combines AI productivity gains with locked-in revenue; Lightspeed's thesis worth studying.

### Patterns that broke

- **Bench's services-heavy bookkeeping** burned $135M before collapse. Do not mistake your AI accounting firm for a "labor co with software wrapper" if relying on venture capital.
- **Pilot's services-led approach** has stalled at $1.2B valuation for ~5 years despite $222M raised. Services margins cap valuation.

### Most active investors to know

- **Tier 1 highly active in AI accounting:** a16z, Sequoia, Lightspeed, Accel, GV, General Catalyst, Bessemer, Goldman Sachs Growth, ICONIQ, IVP, Index Ventures.
- **Strategic capital entering with distribution leverage:** Thomson Reuters Ventures (Truewind, Fieldguide), Money Forward (Fondo), Microsoft ScaleUp (Karbon), NVIDIA partnership (Digits), Goldman Sachs (Fieldguide), KKR (Aiwyn).
- **Emerging specialists frequently leading at Seed/A:** Costanoa, F-Prime, Standard Capital.

### Quick-read positioning summary

- **Most crowded zones (10+ funded players each):** SMB AI bookkeeping firms, AP/AR automation, audit/SOX.
- **Best-funded post-YC plays:** Campfire (~$103M), Klarity ($90M), Numeral (~$57M), Sphere (~$26M), Concourse ($17M), Truewind ($17M).
- **Two unicorns minted in 2026 on AI-accounting theses:** Basis ($1.15B) and Pennylane ($4.25B). Fieldguide is at $700M, on the runway.
