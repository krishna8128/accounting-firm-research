Basis (getbasis.ai)

The $1.15B AI-agent platform for US Top 25 accounting firms. Founded NYC 2023 by two non-accountants from Goldman/Puzzle and Harvard/BCG. Why they're winning, what they're really selling, and what it means for an Indian bootstrap founder.

TL;DR — Six Things to Know About Basis

1. Capital scale: $138M raised in 28 months, $1.15B valuation. Seed $3.6M Oct '23 (Better Tomorrow) → Series A $34M Dec '24 (Khosla/Rabois) → Series B $100M Feb '26 (Accel + GV). Angels include Lloyd Blankfein (former Goldman CEO), Jeff Dean (Google DeepMind), Noam Brown (OpenAI o1 lead), Adam D'Angelo, Aaron Levie, Clem Delangue, Amjad Masad. This is the most heavily-funded AI accounting company in the world.
2. Founders are NOT accountants. Matt Harpe (CEO): Harvard → BCG → SoftBank Vision Fund. Mitch Troyanovsky (co-founder): Babson Finance → Goldman PM → Puzzle PM. Classic "smart finance/consulting operator who saw the pain" archetype — closer to Ramp/Brex template than to Karbon/FloQast template. Mitch's stint at Puzzle (an AI bookkeeping startup) is the unique edge: he saw the accounting workflow up close before founding.
3. The wedge: long-horizon AI agents for US Top 25 accounting firms, across CAS + Tax + Audit + Advisory. Hero demo: an autonomous agent that completes a 1065 partnership tax return end-to-end. ~30% of US Top 25 firms and ~20% of Top 150 are customers. Named: Baker Tilly (Top 10), Wiss, UHY, BPB, Boulay, Clark Nuber, MarksNelson, Pinion, WilkinGuttenplan, Abacus CPAs.
4. Sales motion: forward-deployed engineers (Palantir-style), 6+ month enterprise pilots. Basis hires a "Deployed Intelligence" team that "parachutes into customer organizations" to redesign workflows around their agents. Enterprise contract pricing — never disclosed publicly. Sales role compensation ranges suggest very high ACVs (likely $200K–$1M+ per Top-25 firm). This is the OPPOSITE of self-serve.
5. OpenAI-exclusive stack — single-vendor dependency. Built on OpenAI o3, o3-Pro, GPT-4.1, GPT-5. No public mention of Anthropic/Claude or any other model provider. They run their own evals (claim 100% on internal tool-calling benchmark with GPT-5). Whether this is a strategic advantage (deep integration) or a strategic risk (single-vendor) depends on your view of OpenAI's trajectory.
6. What this means for you (Indian bootstrap founder): Basis is the photo-negative of what you should build. They sell to NYC enterprise buyers with 6-month pilots, $138M war chest, US-only tax forms (1040/1065/1120), and forward-deployed engineering services. You have weeks-to-revenue, India network, no accounting credential, no enterprise capital. Their playbook does not transfer. But their existence creates a different opportunity for you — see the takeaway section.

Product — What Basis Actually Ships

Basis evolved from two named products (Basis Intern + Basis Accountant) at seed stage into a unified multi-agent platform. The architecture is supervisor-and-sub-agents on OpenAI's reasoning models.

Sales Motion & Pricing

Sales motion

  • Direct enterprise. Job board shows: Enterprise AE, Sales Leader, Sales Manager, GTM Associate, Revenue Ops Leader, Intelligence Architect, Senior Engineering Recruiter.
  • "Deployed Intelligence" forward-deployed engineering team. Basis calls them people who "parachute into customer organizations, learn their unique context, and give them the tools to redesign their accounting workflows around intelligent agents." This is the Palantir/Scale AI services model.
  • Pilot path (Wiss model): Beta in one practice area (CAS) → 6+ months → full practice rollout → expand to other practice areas. Multi-quarter sales cycle.
  • Domain credibility via Kenji Kuramoto (Managing Partner In-Residence, ex-Acuity founder) — opens conversations with firm partners.
  • Strategic accounting-firm LPs in seed round — likely provided early design partners and warm intros.

Pricing (inferred — none publicly disclosed)

  • LOW No published pricing anywhere — website, G2, Capterra, reviews all blank.
  • Almost certainly enterprise contract pricing, given (a) sales-led role mix, (b) "parachute into customer organizations" deployment, (c) Top-25-firm customer base.
  • Sales-role compensation listings reference $100K–$400K total comp for AE roles — implies large ACVs and meaningful commissions.
  • Likely ACV range: $200K–$1M+ per firm for Top-25 tier; possibly lower for regional firms.
  • Math check: $1.15B valuation ÷ assume 15× ARR multiple = ~$77M ARR target. ÷ ~30 customers = ~$2.5M average ACV. That's a stretch — they're either still ramping ARR or the multiple is much higher (40–60×). More likely the latter given valuation = bet on category dominance.
Forward-deployed engineering is a strategic choice with serious implications. It produces high contract values, deep moats, and customer love — but it also makes the business look more like a services firm than a software firm at gross-margin level. Palantir wore this as a feature; many SaaS investors hate it. The bet: deep customer integration → unmovable from the stack → expansion revenue → eventually self-serve from the data flywheel. Worked for Palantir at scale; many imitators stalled at $20–50M ARR.

Strategic Narrative — Three Load-Bearing Ideas

1. Long-horizon agents, not copilots

"It's closer to delegating an entire engagement to a team member than asking a chatbot a question." Mitch Troyanovsky — Bloomberg / SiliconANGLE, Feb 2026

This is the central product differentiator. Basis explicitly contrasts itself with "AI copilots" (suggested by every incumbent). They claim agents that run autonomously for hours, with future potential for days or weeks. The 1065 tax return demo is the proof point.

2. Doers into reviewers

"Top accounting firms use Basis across CAS, Tax, and Audit to turn their doers into reviewers and expand revenue capacity." Basis marketing copy

This is the firm-economics pitch — and it's brilliant. Not "we replace your accountants" (which terrifies firm partners). Instead: "same headcount, more billable engagements, better margins." It directly addresses the CPA shortage. Claimed efficiency: 20–50% across practices; ~30% time savings most-cited.

3. Agent-native organization

"The companies that thrive in the AGI era won't be the ones that use AI the most. They'll be the ones that reshape themselves to work with agents most effectively." Basis blog — "Building a Company for the AGI Era"

Basis positions itself not just as a product but as the organizational prototype for an agent-first company. Atlas (internal "agents-building-agents" team) is the concrete proof. Keith Rabois publicly calls them "the agent-native organization of 2030." This is recruiting + investor narrative as much as product.

Narrative discipline is itself a moat. All three messages reinforce each other and have been consistent from seed announcement (Oct 2023) through Series B (Feb 2026). They tell partners "we make you more profitable," tell investors "we're the platform play," and tell engineers "you'll build something existential." That's BCG-trained storytelling working as designed.

The competitors Basis founders actually worry about (inferred)

  • Firms' own in-house AI builds. Big 4 + large mid-tier firms have CIOs hiring ML teams. Wiss uses both Basis AND private ChatGPT.
  • Big 4 internal platforms. Deloitte Zora, PwC agent OS, EY.ai, KPMG Clara are all enterprise AI builds that could leak into Top 50 firm market.
  • Thomson Reuters + Wolters Kluwer. They own the tax/audit content moat (CCH, GoSystem, CoCounsel) and are aggressively adding agents. Materia acquisition shows TR will buy what they can't build.
  • Intuit/Xero. Less direct — they serve SMBs, not Top 25 firms — but their data integration with QBO clients of these firms creates a different angle of attack.

Vulnerabilities & Open Questions

  • Single-model dependency. OpenAI-exclusive stack. If OpenAI raises prices, has an outage, or loses to Anthropic/Google on the next reasoning generation, Basis has no fallback architecture publicly disclosed.
  • Integration opacity. No public CCH/TR/Karbon/Workday/NetSuite/Intacct integrations confirmed. Likely heavy per-customer custom work via Deployed Intelligence team. Scales linearly with engineering capacity, not exponentially.
  • Forward-deployed model = high services intensity. Gross margins likely below pure-SaaS comps. Investors who reward 75%+ GM will be unhappy if Basis stays in 50–65% range.
  • "30% time savings" is modest compared to vertical specialists. Black Ore claims 98% time savings on 1040s. If a firm chooses one tool per practice area, vertical specialists may win individual practice budgets.
  • No Big 4 customer disclosed. Big 4 are explicitly building internally (Deloitte Zora, PwC agent OS, EY.ai, KPMG Clara). The Top 100 firms below them are the addressable wedge — but TAM caps faster than Accel's $1.15B valuation suggests.
  • Customer concentration. ~30 customers carry $1.15B valuation. Loss of 2–3 Top 25 logos to a competitor (or to a firm's internal build) would dent ARR materially.
  • 1.4× valuation jump per year. $34M @ undisclosed val Dec '24 → $100M @ $1.15B Feb '26. If Series A was at ~$350–500M (reasonable Khosla guess), the Series B multiple is ~3× — aggressive. Forward multiples on ARR are likely 30–60×. The company has to deliver step-function ARR growth in 2026–27 to grow into the valuation.
  • No international play disclosed. Product is shaped around US tax forms (1040/1065/1120). Expansion into Canada/UK/AU/India is unaddressed — both an opportunity and a competitor gap that someone else can exploit.

Three lessons to extract from Basis

Lesson 1 — Frame "AI replaces the work, not the workers." Basis's "doers into reviewers" framing is genius for a reason: firm partners don't want to fire their staff; they want bigger margins. Steal this framing for your wedge. If you're selling to Indian CA firms or India BPOs, the message is "your articleship/junior staff become reviewers; the AI does the prep work."
Lesson 2 — Pick a hero demo that's IMPOSSIBLE to fake. Their 1065 partnership tax return demo is genius because (a) it's specific, (b) it's hard, (c) every Top-25-firm partner has done one and knows what good looks like. Your wedge needs an equivalent hero demo. "AI does GST reconciliation for 50 GSTINs across a single client in 30 min" or "AI matches 100 vendor invoices to PO + GRN in 5 min" are candidates. Specific. Hard. Recognizable.
Lesson 3 — Investor signaling is also customer signaling. Basis's strategic LPs in seed were "several top accounting firms." That gave them early design partners + credibility from day one. For your version: get 2–3 mid-tier Indian CA firms or BPOs as advisors/angels first, before building. Their endorsement is worth more than capital.

The specific window Basis leaves open for you

Basis explicitly does NOT play in any of the following spaces — every one of them is a wedge you could win:

  1. Indian SMBs (60M+ businesses). GST/TDS/ITR + Tally-anchored workflow. Basis can't read Tally; doesn't understand GST.
  2. Indian CA firms serving Indian clients. ~1.4 lakh CA firms; mostly small (1–10 partners); zero AI tooling that fits their workflow.
  3. India BPOs serving US firms. Entigrity (200K offshore accountants) and peers are doing the junior execution work that Basis automates ABOVE — meaning Basis customers still use offshore BPOs. AI for the BPO's execution layer is wide-open.
  4. Small US firms (Top 500–5000). Below Basis's Top-150 ICP. Aiwyn/Karbon serve some; the long tail is mostly untouched.
  5. Non-US, non-India markets. Canada, UK, AU, EU, Latin America — Basis doesn't ship there.
  6. Vertical specialists (R&D credit, transfer pricing, expat tax, sales-and-use). Basis is horizontal — these high-ACV niches are open.

Of these, the three that fit your specific constraints (Indian-based, tech background, bootstrap, 6-month revenue) are: #1 (Indian SMBs), #2 (Indian CA firms), #3 (India BPOs serving US firms). Pick based on which your warm network actually points to — the question we left open in the chat.

The single most useful thing Basis tells you about your own play: A $1.15B AI accounting company exists in the SF/NYC bubble, employs 141 people, and yet has zero presence in any of the markets you can actually reach. That's not a threat. That's a starting gun.
