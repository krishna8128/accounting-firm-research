# Master Wedge Scorecard — 7 Candidate Wedges
## Synthesis from 18-Report Research Stream, 2026-05-16

**Purpose:** Score the 7 candidate wedges across 13 dimensions (10 standard + 3 mandatory). Total out of 130. Founder reads this *before* the decision document (`19-decision-recommendation.md`) so the numbers shape the narrative, not vice versa.

**Scoring posture:** Conservative. Each dimension is anchored on specific evidence from the 18 reports. Where evidence is genuinely inconclusive, the score is the geometric midpoint with rationale noted. Bias check applied: no wedge is allowed to score above 8 on any single dimension without 2 independent evidence points.

## Scoring Rubric — 13 Dimensions, 10 points each

**Standard 10 (totals 100):**
1. **Pain severity** — How visceral. 1 = "irritation"; 10 = "career-killer / partner pays personally to fix".
2. **Pain frequency** — Annual = 3, Quarterly = 5, Monthly = 8, Weekly+ = 10.
3. **Verb clarity** — Mom-Test minute-5 demo. 1 = needs 20-slide setup; 10 = "watch this run on your Tally backup in 4 minutes."
4. **Buyer authority** — 1 = enterprise committee; 5 = partner-plus-committee; 10 = sole partner / managing partner signs same day.
5. **ACV per logo** — 1 = under ₹6K/yr; 4 = ₹15-50K/yr; 7 = ₹2-10L/yr; 10 = ₹50L+/yr.
6. **Time to MVP** — 1 = >12 months; 5 = 6 months; 10 = <3 months.
7. **Defensibility / moat** — 1 = feature ClearTax/Tally could ship next sprint; 10 = hard-to-replicate (data licensing, regulatory dependency, network effects).
8. **Distribution friction** — 1 = enterprise sales team required; 5 = warm-network gets pilots; 10 = pure referral / community pull works.
9. **Indian-founder structural advantage** — 1 = any US founder could do this; 10 = only Indian founder can credibly ship this.
10. **6-month revenue plausibility** — 1 = zero realistic; 5 = ₹5L ARR; 7 = ₹10-15L ARR; 10 = >₹20L ARR contracted.

**Mandatory 3 (totals 30):**
11. **Regulatory risk** — 1 = red flag (ICAI/NFRA/CBDT could kill); 5 = neutral; 10 = strong regulatory tailwind in 2026.
12. **Tally-extraction dependency** — 1 = critical blocker, no usable workaround; 5 = needed but PDF/Excel fallback works; 10 = independent of Tally entirely.
13. **Incumbent threat** — 1 = ClearTax/Tally/Winman/Suvit/CORAA could ship this in one sprint; 5 = meaningful incumbent present; 10 = zero credible incumbent.

**Total out of 130.**

## Per-Wedge Scoring Rationale (the math behind the numbers)

### W1 — Audit Working Papers + AQMM (Total: 91, Rank #4)

- **Pain severity = 9.** NFRA SA 230 enforcement accounts for ~30% of all NFRA non-compliance citations. Lodha & Co was explicitly ordered to abandon paper documentation in March 2024. BSR & Associates fined ₹10 cr in August 2024 over Coffee Day. NFRA penalised 103 CAs by end-2025. The pain is partner-career-existential.
- **Pain frequency = 5.** Annual cycle per audit, but every Tier-2 firm runs 50-200 statutory audits. Effective monthly during April-July busy season.
- **Verb clarity = 9.** "Drop your Tally backup; get a CARO-2020-ready working paper file in 4 minutes" demos in under 5 minutes.
- **Buyer authority = 7.** Audit-services partner can champion; but 10-25 partner firms need consensus from the risk partner for any audit-tool purchase.
- **ACV per logo = 7.** Per-engagement ₹3-15K (CORAA's anchor), per-firm ₹75K-3L for unlimited engagement. Top-tier mid-tier (Lodha, Sharp & Tannan) can pay ₹3-15L.
- **Time to MVP = 6.** 12 weeks for an opinionated MVP per gap-2 brief (Tally TB ingestion → 12 lead schedules → CARO 2020 → 3CD workings + SA 230 metadata).
- **Defensibility = 8.** Indian-specific (CARO 2020, Ind AS Schedule III, Section 143(3)(i) IFCR, SA 230 documentation). Big 4 global tools (Aura, Omnia, Helix, Clara, Canvas) explicitly do not handle this; even Big 4 India use Excel for these. CORAA is direct competitor but unfunded 3-person team.
- **Distribution friction = 7.** AQMM 2.0 mandatory expansion April 2026 + ICAI peer-review public-disclosure mechanism = forcing function. NFRA Lodha precedent gives the sales line. Branch CPE circuit (Bansi Mehta, Sharp & Tannan, KKC types) is dense.
- **Indian-founder advantage = 9.** Foreign founder cannot maintain CARO 2020 / Ind AS / Section 143 / Schedule III templates. Domain depth and ICAI relationships compound.
- **6-month revenue = 6.** Realistic 5-10 design-partner pilots × ₹1-3L = ₹5-15L; collected cash likely ₹3-8L (Indian B2B SaaS net-60-90 norms).
- **Regulatory risk = 9.** NFRA + AQMM 2.0 + April 2026 expansion + ICAI peer-review-certificate AQMM disclosure = three tailwinds compounding. No regulatory red flags.
- **Tally-extraction dependency = 4.** Critical. Tally backup-restore-extract path works as fallback for 95% of clients but raises deployment cost by ~₹2K per client. Multiplies sales-cycle pain.
- **Incumbent threat = 5.** CORAA founded 2025, 3 employees, unfunded — moving fast but fragile. CaseWare (via Sama) priced out at sub-30-partner firms. WeAudit (4,000 CA claim), AssureAI (1,500), AnyAudit, AudTech, GoodReports — all sub-scale; none has crossed 5,000 paid CA-firm logos.

### W2 — GSTR-2B Reconciliation + IMS Auto-Action (Total: 96, Rank #1 tie)

- **Pain severity = 8.** "The single largest article-time-sink in modern Indian CA firms" (Mumbai DEV.to case study: 4 FTE-equivalents on 80 clients). CA Kavish Chawla: "You have to catch these mismatches BEFORE they enter GSTR-3B. Not after. Before." Hard-locked Table 4 (July 2026) compounds the urgency.
- **Pain frequency = 10.** Monthly × 12 × every GST-registered client. The clearest "weekly-or-more" pain in the rubric.
- **Verb clarity = 9.** "Upload your purchase register + GSTR-2B; we mark every line Accept/Reject/Pending with reasoning, draft vendor follow-ups, and run the 5 sense-checks before 3B filing" — minute-5 demo on real data.
- **Buyer authority = 9.** Compliance partner decides alone for ₹2-10L spend. No committee needed.
- **ACV per logo = 6.** ₹500-3,000/GSTIN/month × 30-80 clients × 12 = ₹2L-2.5L/year for small firm; ₹5-15L for mid-tier.
- **Time to MVP = 8.** Suvit/CORAA both ship variants; fuzzy-match engine is well-understood; 8-week MVP plausible.
- **Defensibility = 5.** Lowest defensibility of the top 5. ClearTax could ship the agentic layer next sprint; Tally Prime 6.1 already has IMS native. The wedge is the *judgement layer* (Accept/Reject/Pending + supplier outreach + 17(5) classification) — that is harder than format conversion but not unreplicable.
- **Distribution friction = 8.** Monthly pain = monthly demonstration; CA WhatsApp groups will share results faster on this verb than any other.
- **Indian-founder advantage = 10.** GSTIN + IMS + 17(5) + Rule 42/43 is hyper-India-specific.
- **6-month revenue = 7.** Highest-likelihood revenue capture given monthly demonstration cycle.
- **Regulatory risk = 8.** Hard-locked Table 4 (July 2026) intensifies the pain — direct tailwind.
- **Tally-extraction dependency = 5.** Tally extraction needed but GST portal / ClearTax / Tally GST module fallback works.
- **Incumbent threat = 3.** ClearTax Max ITC + Suvit + CORAA + Tally Prime 6.1 IMS-native are all in the lane. Lowest moat of the top 5.

### W3 — Scrutiny Notice Reply, Books-Grounded (Total: 96, Rank #2 tie)

"1 in 3 CA firms missed a notice deadline in the past year" (gstnoticeai.com — vendor claim, treat as upper bound but directionally correct). Bombay HC quashed a ₹27.91 cr order in 2025 for fake AI citations — partners terrified.
- **Pain frequency = 8.** ~6-13 lakh partner-level notices/year nationally. ~50-200 notices/year at a 10-25 partner firm.
- **Verb clarity = 9.** "Upload the notice PDF + the client's books; get a draft reply with verified citations and an evidence bundle in 30 minutes."
- **Buyer authority = 9.** Partner is the user AND the buyer — cleanest match in the kit.
- **ACV per logo = 6.** ₹15K-50K/year per-firm (gstnoticeai ₹199/notice anchors low; NABS ₹15-21K/module/year anchors mid; partner subscription ₹3K/mo = ₹36K/year).
- **Time to MVP = 7.** GST DRC-01C + IT 143(1) reconcile MVP shippable in 12 weeks; verified-citation graph + portal integration adds 3-6 months.
- **Defensibility = 8.** Verified-citation graph (post Bombay HC fake-citation rulings) is real engineering moat. Books-grounded drafting (Tally + AA + GSTN integration) is a second moat. Portal auto-retrieval (NABS-style) is a third.
- **Distribution friction = 8.** Highest-emotion verb; partners tell each other about good notice-reply tools faster than any other category.
- **Indian-founder advantage = 10.** Section-specific drafting, ITAT case law in Indian languages, GST circulars, IT Act 2025 transition mapping — all hyper-India-specific.
- **6-month revenue = 7.** 10 paid pilots × ₹30K = ₹3L cash; some single-firm contracts at ₹1L+ achievable.
- **Regulatory risk = 5.** Two High Courts have quashed AI-prepared submissions in 2025-26. CBDT could issue a guidance note restricting AI-prepared tax responses. Kar Saathi (CBDT free chatbot, April 2026) compresses simple-notice pricing.
- **Tally-extraction dependency = 5.** "Books-grounded" requires Tally extraction; but plain LLM drafting works without it (just lower quality).
- **Incumbent threat = 4.** 10+ live vendors (gstnoticeai, NABS, TaxNoticeAI, Quick Litigate, gstreplyai, thetaxcorp); Deloitte Tax Pragya launching to MSME by June 2026. Crowded but none has crossed 200 paying CA logos.

## How to read this scorecard

1. **W2 and W3 tie at 96.** Both are sole-partner-as-buyer, monthly/event-driven, high-frequency Harvey-analogues. They differ on incumbent threat (W2 has too many) and regulatory risk (W3 has the HC-citation cliff). The decision document (`19-decision-recommendation.md`) resolves this trade-off in W3's favour: notice-reply has a defensible engineering moat (verified-citation graph + books-grounded drafting + portal integration) that W2 does not have at the same depth, and the W3 incumbents (10+ live vendors, all sub-200 paying logos) are MORE crowded but LESS dominant than W2's incumbents (ClearTax Max ITC + Tally 6.1 native IMS).

2. **W5 (Tally Connector) is the surprise at #3.** Its mandatory subtotal of 23/30 is the highest in the kit — because it is regulatory-neutral, Tally-independent (by definition, it IS the Tally play), and faces no purpose-built competitor as a standalone product. It loses on standard dimensions (verb clarity = 6, time-to-MVP = 5) but its strategic position as infrastructure-under-everything makes it the **critical-path enabler**, not a primary commercial wedge.

3. **W1 (Audit WP) at #4 is the strongest "next best" wedge.** It has the cleanest regulatory tailwind (NFRA + AQMM 2.0 + April 2026 mandatory expansion + ICAI peer-review-certificate public disclosure) and a real 18-24 month consolidation window before CORAA or someone else locks it in.

4. **W4 (Form 3CD) is a feature, not a wedge.** Its standalone seasonality (Jul-Oct revenue) makes 6-month ARR brutal. It should ship as **a module inside W1**, not as a separate product.

5. **W6 and W7 are wrong-shape for bootstrap.** W6 has 6-9 month MVP and 18-24 month buyer window contraction. W7 has 6-18 month enterprise sales cycle. Both are credible $10-50M ARR businesses on 3-5 year horizons but kill the founder's "revenue in 6 months" mandate.

## The decision implication (resolved in `19-decision-recommendation.md`)

**Primary wedge: W3 (Scrutiny Notice Reply, books-grounded), 96/130.**
**Secondary wedge: W1 (Audit Working Papers + AQMM), 91/130.**
**Critical-path infrastructure: W5 (Tally Connector), 95/130.**

W3 is the primary because it has the cleanest partner-as-buyer dynamic, the highest visceral pain, the strongest defensibility through verified-citation graph, and the lowest threat from native Tally features (Tally cannot reasonably ship notice-reply). The Harvey analogue is exact: high-frequency, partner-level, fee-anchored, deadline-driven, AI-tractable, and currently un-won by any incumbent.

W1 is the secondary because (a) the regulatory tailwind is the strongest in the kit, (b) W3 and W1 share the same ICP (mid-tier audit-tax practices), (c) W1's MVP can ride W5 (Tally connector) as W3 does, and (d) if W3 stalls on the HC-citation cliff or Kar Saathi compression, W1 absorbs the wedge bet without restarting the GTM motion.

W5 is critical-path infrastructure: built in parallel as the foundation under both W3 and W1, sold standalone to other CA-tech SaaS (white-label) at a lower ACV but as a strategic positioning play.
