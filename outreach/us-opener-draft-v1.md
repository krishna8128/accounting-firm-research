# US cold outreach to CPA firm owners — draft v1

**Status:** v1 draft, untested. Iterate after first 10 sends.
Context in `memory/outreach_state_may_2026.md` and `memory/phase_metrics_may_2026.md`.

**Audience:** Owners / managing partners of US CPA firms. Target tier TBD — likely mid-market ($1M–$10M revenue, 5–50 employees) where AI ROI is real but they're underserved by Big-4-targeted enterprise tools.

**Channel:** LinkedIn DM primary, email secondary.

**Positioning constraint:** US CPA owners filter cold outreach ruthlessly. Curiosity ("can I pick your brain?") gets ignored. Must lead with concrete observation, signal homework, low ask. India-style "young founder researching" hook does not transfer.

---

## Variant A — observation-first (use AFTER ~5 India calls)

**First line (LinkedIn) / Subject (email):**
> Saw [Firm Name] focuses on [specific service area or industry vertical from their site]. Quick question about how owners in that space are thinking about AI right now.

**Body:**
> Hey [First Name] — I've been talking to owners of mid-sized accounting firms over the past few weeks to understand where AI is actually moving the needle vs. where it's hype. Two patterns I keep hearing: [PATTERN 1 from interview data], and [PATTERN 2 from interview data].
>
> I'm a founder researching this space — no product yet, not selling anything. Would value 15 min of your perspective on what's been useful and what's been noise inside your firm. If a call is too much, happy to send 3 questions by email.
>
> — Krishna

**Why this works:** Specific firm reference signals homework. "Two patterns I keep hearing" creates intrigue and positions Krishna as someone with field data. Explicit "no product, no selling" disarms the filter. 3-question fallback lowers ask further.

**Why this might fail:** Until we have real patterns from India conversations to drop in, this is generic. **Hold until we have at least 5 India calls done.**

---

## Variant B — research-first (USE NOW)

**First line / Subject:**
> Researching how US firms in the [X–Y headcount] segment are evaluating AI right now — would value your perspective.

**Body:**
> Hey [First Name] — I'm a founder doing primary research with owners of US accounting firms in the [size range] range. I'm trying to map what's actually solving real problems vs. what's demo-ware in firms your size.
>
> Not selling — no product yet. The trade I can offer: I'll share what other owners in your peer group are telling me about AI tools they've tried (anonymized). 15 min on your calendar this week or next, or 3 written questions by email if that's easier.
>
> — Krishna

**Why this works:** Honest about research-mode positioning. Trade ("I learn, you also get value") changes the frame from one-sided ask. Owner peer-data is genuinely valuable — most US CPA owners don't know what their peers are doing with AI.

**Why this might fail:** "I'm doing research" is the most common cold-outreach lie. Owners have heard it 100 times. Needs a credibility anchor — even one line like "background: built tooling for [X] for 3 years" would help. **Open question for Krishna: do we have anything?**

---

## Variant C — provocation (hold for v2)

**Subject:**
> One pattern I keep hearing from US CPA firm owners about AI

**Body:**
> Hey [First Name] —
>
> Talking to owners of firms in the [N]-person range, the most common AI complaint I hear: "every vendor pitches the same thing and none of it touches the work that actually slows us down."
>
> Curious if that matches your experience — or if the bottleneck for you is somewhere completely different.
>
> Researching the space — no product, not pitching. 15 min this week or a reply email both work.
>
> — Krishna

**Why this works:** Provocation triggers reply ("yes, exactly" or "no, the real problem is X"). Both responses are high-signal.

**Why this might fail:** Provocation requires the claim to land. If wrong, we look ignorant. Currently plausible but unvalidated — promote to active variant once we have data.
